How 14 salaries work in Austria
Austrian law splits salary into two separate tax regimes:

Regular salary (§66 EStG) — 12× per year
Your monthly salary is annualised at ×12 and taxed using the progressive Lohnsteuer brackets (0–55%). Social insurance (SV) applies at full rates every month.

Sonderzahlungen (§67 EStG) — 2× per year
The 13th salary (Urlaubsgeld, typically June) and 14th salary (Weihnachtsgeld, typically November) are taxed at a flat 6% — only on the amount above the €620 Freibetrag. SV applies normally. This flat rate is significantly lower than your marginal bracket (likely 30–40%), which is why the SZ are very tax-efficient.

The key rule: Lohnsteuer on regular monthly salary is always calculated using ×12 annualisation — never ×14. The SZ are calculated and taxed entirely separately.
